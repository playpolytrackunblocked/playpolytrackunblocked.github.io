What Is PolyTrack?

PolyTrack is a low-poly racing game inspired by time-trial track builders, where every loop, landing, and restart exists to shave milliseconds off your best line. On this page, the goal is to make that browser session easier to access and easier to understand, especially for players searching for a clean unblocked version.

The official PolyTrack page highlights two big ideas: speed and creativity. You can attack existing tracks for a cleaner time, then switch into the editor and build something of your own to share.

That mix of racing and construction is what gives PolyTrack its staying power. It is not just a game about beating one course and moving on; it is a game about understanding flow, lines, and how a track should feel under pressure.

How to Play PolyTrack

Your main goal is to finish quickly and refine each run until your route feels efficient. Hitting jumps cleanly, carrying speed through corners, and restarting without hesitation are all part of the game.

Kodub lists keyboard controls directly on the official itch.io page, with WASD or arrow keys for driving and R or Enter to restart. That instant restart loop is a huge part of PolyTrack's speedrun appeal.

PC / Browser Controls

  • Drive with WASD or the arrow keys.
  • Press R or Enter to restart the track quickly.

Mobile Notes

  • The official project also lists Android support, but the browser version is still the cleanest way to focus on precision driving.

PolyTrack Tips and Strategy

PolyTrack gets better once you stop treating each attempt like a single race and start treating it like a series of experiments. Small adjustments in approach angle or braking point can dramatically change your time.

  • Restart early when a line is obviously lost instead of carrying a bad run to the finish.
  • Learn how the car settles after jumps so you can enter the next section already prepared.
  • Use the editor mindset even while racing: think about why the best line works.
